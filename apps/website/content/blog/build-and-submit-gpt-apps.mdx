OpenAI's ChatGPT Apps allow you to integrate your application directly with users inside ChatGPT. In this guide, we'll walk through the complete process from building your app to publishing it in the OpenAI directory.

Before diving in, review [OpenAI's App Submission Guidelines](https://developers.openai.com/apps-sdk/app-submission-guidelines) to ensure your app complies with their safety requirements and prohibitions. Once you have your idea, you'll choose from three possible approaches: a text-only app, a React components app, or a full Next.js app.

In this guide, we'll build a simple yet fully integrated React app. By the end, you'll have a working application submitted to the OpenAI directory.

## Submission
Go to the OpenAI Platform Dashboard, there you will find the ChatGPT Apps section, one you are there go to create.It will present you a form for completion, some of the most important fields, besides logo and name, are the following:
- Subtitle -> this will be the best way to make your tool discoverable
- Description -> make it the most marketing way, but structured and understandable, so every user understand the full scope of your app
- Demo video, this will be displayed once the user clicks on your app at the directory, the user will be more likely to watch the video rather than read the description, show all the use cases and make sure to show them in the best possible way.
- App Commerce and purchasing, if your app involves sales but remember they should be physical good, you will need to check this.
Afterward, you will need to provide the link to your server, you will verify if it requires auth or not and it will scan for tools. If it detects them, it will ask you to justify the annotations added to your tools, and to add a ver

If you fetch from other places csp should be there.Before submitting your app, we encourage you to read the guidelines around safety and privacy for GPT Apps
